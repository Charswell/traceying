Ole Smoky

Yes, legal moonshine is a thing, and Ole Smoky is the only jar to unlid when you’re looking to try some. Since most people associate moonshine with backwoods bathtubs, it was our job to educate our audience about how and when to drink it. Most importantly, we were tasked with getting them to add moonshine to their usual alcohol lineup (namely whiskey, vodka, gin, beer, wine, or any other alcoholic beverage you would reach for before white lightnin’.) The below content is our take on introducing different flavors, and showing what shaking up the norm can do for a wild night.

Scorch Trails

The Task:
Get teenagers to buy DVDs. Our job was to increase awareness and drive sales of the Home Video Release of The Scorch Trials by engaging fans and leveraging value added material. The challenge was twofold: Make Scorch Trials stand out in the crowded “Dystopian-YA-book-to-movies” space, and establish Scorch Trials as its own brand entity within the larger Maze Runner franchise in order to sell the deluxe DVD package.

The Approach:
While concepting the campaign, there were over 700k Maze Runner-related hashtag uses on Instagram. We capitalized on this existing interest by “infecting” Scorch Trial fans on Instagram with The Flare (which is a grossdisease from the movie). Once infected, fans had to post a selfie using the hashtag #CureTheFlare to receive the cure via a personalized Instagram Direct message. We also declared specific fans “immune” and offered them an exclusive first-look at VAM (Value-Added Material) content.

The Results:
The account that did the infecting, @wckd_labs, received 21.6k followers (we started the account anew when the activation began). Content engagements on the account exceeded 104k, and total engagement on content from the campaign on the existing @mazerunnermovie account was over 147k. #CureTheFlare was used almost 2k times on Instagram and 12k times on Twitter. To top it all off, fans didn’t just send selfies: They sent themselves “Flare-ified,” leveling up our ask with even more rabid affinity for the activation.

Spotless

The Task: Position Esquire Network’s first scripted series as premium content worthy of water cooler conversation. As a dark drama, the show had steep competition, vying for L+3 tune-in from a target audience that prefers VOD, and on a network that isn’t perceived as a destination for dramatic content.

1. The Approach: The Voice


The Content: We created posts that introduce fans to the world of a mob boss and his crime scene cleaner. We leveraged the dark, deviant nature of the show to disrupting fans’ news feed with shocking content.


The Results: Within the first two weeks of posting content, our audience grew by 94% on Facebook, 10% on Twitter, and 6,000% on Instagram. When it came to using our unique content style to drive pre-linear launch, we drove 9,655 link clicks and 3.5m impressions. And all thanks to our doing (exclusively our doing and no one else’s), the show was renewed for Season 2!

Angie Tribeca

The Task:
Angie Tribeca was one of the shows released after TBS rebranded themselves as the new home of smart comedy without rules. It was on us to create a smart, self-aware social campaign. We matched the spooftastic*, satirical tone of the show to position Angie Tribeca as the breakout series of the year, further positioning TBS as the new home of smart comedy without rules.

*spooftastic was Steve Carell’s word. He was the show’s executive producer. You’re probably beginning to understand that this was account was a dream.

Pre-Premiere: We leveraged the star-studded cast to spoof other show and brand campaigns, promote the show in an overly dramatic tone, and declare the show’s destined race for 2017 Golden Globe nomination.

ESQUIRE

The task: We were tasked with raising awareness and driving tune-in for a network with very low name recognition, and positioning Esquire the playbook for championing a well-played life. (Their words, not mine.)

The approach: Of content that the network had already been posting, lifestyle content outperformed. And because across social, conversational & relatable content over-indexes, our initial content calendar aimed to discover the moments fans would rally around the most, and that the brand could talk about authentically. Then, we put the Esquire spin on these topics. For example, we found out our audience likes sports. But if they wanted the scores they would go to ESPN. Instead, we aimed to find the smarter, more clever angle on topics from sports to style.

The results: Our lifestyle-driven content outperformed all show content, receiving higher average impressions and engagement rate. On Facebook, the difference was most dramatic, with lifestyle content garnering nearly 1% ER and over 140K impressions, while show content received .25% visible ER and 20K impressions.
